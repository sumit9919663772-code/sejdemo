Acne & Pimples

Acne is a common skin problem that occurs when the pores of the skin become clogged with excess oil (sebum), dead skin cells, and bacteria. As a result, pimples, whiteheads, blackheads, and sometimes painful red bumps develop on the skin.

Psoriasis

Psoriasis is a chronic (long-term) skin condition in which skin cells build up too quickly, leading to the formation of thick, red patches covered with silvery-white scales. It is not contagious, but it can be uncomfortable and sometimes painful.

Get More Info

Eczema

Eczema is a chronic inflammatory skin condition that causes the skin to become dry, itchy, red, and irritated. It often comes in flare-ups, where symptoms worsen for a period and then improve.

Eczema commonly affects the face, hands, elbows, knees, neck, and behind the ears, and it can occur in children as well as adults.

Fungal Infections

Fungal infections are common skin conditions caused by the overgrowth of fungi on the skin. They usually develop in warm, moist areas of the body and can affect people of all ages. These infections are contagious and can spread through direct skin contact or shared items.

Pigmentation

Pigmentation refers to the darkening or uneven coloring of the skin caused by excess production of melanin, the pigment responsible for skin color. It can appear as dark patches, spots, or uneven skin tone on different parts of the body.

Get More Info

Chronic Skin Diseases

Chronic skin diseases are long-term skin conditions that tend to last for months or years and often show periodic flare-ups and remissions. These conditions may not have a permanent cure, but with proper care and management, symptoms can be controlled and reduced.
