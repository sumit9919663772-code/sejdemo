Eczema & Ayurvedic Understanding

Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis) is a chronic skin condition marked by dryness, itching, redness, and irritation. Symptoms often appear in flare-ups and commonly affect the hands, elbows, knees, face, and neck.

What is Eczema?

Eczema, also known as Atopic Dermatitis, is a chronic inflammatory skin condition that causes dryness, itching, redness, and irritation of the skin. It often appears in flare-ups, where symptoms worsen for a period and then improve.

Eczema can affect people of all ages, including children and adults. Commonly affected areas include the hands, elbows, knees, face, neck, and sometimes the scalp. In long-term cases, repeated scratching can make the skin thick, rough, and sensitive.

Key Features of Eczema

  • Persistent itching, often worse at night
  • Dry, rough, or scaly skin
  • Red or inflamed patches
  • Cracked skin that may ooze or bleed
  • Recurring flare-ups and skin sensitivity

Eczema is not contagious, but it requires consistent care and proper management to control symptoms and prevent frequent flare-ups.

Why Does Eczema Occur?

Eczema occurs due to a combination of internal and external factors that weaken the skin’s natural barrier and trigger inflammation. This makes the skin more sensitive to irritants, allergens, and environmental changes.

  • Weak skin barrier: Loss of moisture makes the skin dry and more prone to irritation.
  • Immune system imbalance: An overactive immune response causes inflammation and itching.
  • Genetic factors: A family history of eczema, allergies, or asthma increases the risk.
  • Allergens and irritants: Dust, pollen, soaps, detergents, and chemicals can trigger flare-ups.
  • Environmental factors: Cold weather, dry air, heat, and sweating may worsen symptoms.
  • Stress and lifestyle factors: Emotional stress and unhealthy routines can aggravate eczema.

Since multiple factors are involved, eczema often becomes a long-term condition that requires consistent care and trigger management rather than short-term relief.

What Happens Inside the Body?

In eczema, the body’s internal balance is disturbed, which affects the skin’s natural defense system. The immune response becomes overactive and the skin barrier weakens, making the skin dry, sensitive, and prone to inflammation.

  • Weakened skin barrier: The skin loses moisture easily, leading to dryness and cracking.
  • Overactive immune response: The immune system reacts excessively, causing redness, swelling, and itching.
  • Inflammation inside the skin: Continuous inflammation leads to irritation and flare-ups.
  • Increased nerve sensitivity: Skin nerves become more reactive, resulting in persistent itching.
  • Internal triggers: Stress, digestion issues, and hormonal imbalance can worsen symptoms.

Because eczema involves internal processes and not just surface damage, long-term care focuses on calming inflammation and restoring balance from within.

What To Do During Eczema

According to Ayurveda, eczema develops due to an imbalance of doshas, mainly Pitta and Kapha, which affects skin health and immunity. Management focuses on calming inflammation, soothing the skin, and restoring internal balance.

  • Keep the skin cool and calm: Avoid excessive heat and irritation, as heat aggravates skin inflammation.
  • Maintain regular skin moisturization: Gentle oil-based care helps reduce dryness and itching and supports the skin barrier.
  • Wear loose, breathable clothing: Soft cotton fabrics help reduce friction and prevent irritation.
  • Follow a balanced and light diet: Easily digestible and cooling foods support digestion and skin balance.
  • Manage stress and emotions: Mental stress can disturb dosha balance and worsen skin symptoms.
  • Maintain a disciplined daily routine: Proper sleep, hydration, and routine habits support long-term healing.

Ayurveda emphasizes consistent care and lifestyle balance to support long-term comfort and reduce the recurrence of eczema symptoms.

What To Avoid During Eczema

According to Ayurveda, certain habits, foods, and lifestyle factors can aggravate dosha imbalance and worsen eczema symptoms. Avoiding these triggers helps calm inflammation and supports skin healing.

  • Avoid scratching the skin: Scratching increases irritation, damages the skin barrier, and delays healing.
  • Avoid excessive heat exposure: Hot weather, steam, and hot water baths can aggravate itching and redness.
  • Avoid spicy, oily, and junk foods: Such foods increase internal heat and may worsen skin inflammation.
  • Avoid harsh soaps and chemical products: Strong cleansers disturb the skin’s natural balance and increase dryness.
  • Avoid tight and synthetic clothing: These fabrics trap sweat and increase friction, leading to irritation.
  • Avoid irregular lifestyle habits: Lack of sleep, poor routine, and dehydration can disturb dosha balance.
  • Avoid excessive stress: Mental stress can trigger flare-ups and slow down recovery.

Ayurveda emphasizes prevention through mindful choices. Avoiding these factors helps reduce flare-ups and supports long-term skin comfort.
