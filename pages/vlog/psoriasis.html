Psoriasis

Psoriasis is a chronic, non-contagious skin condition in which skin cells multiply much faster than normal, causing thick, red patches covered with silvery-white scales. It commonly appears on the scalp, elbows, knees, lower back, and sometimes nails, and may be associated with itching, dryness, or discomfort. Psoriasis is not an infection but an immune-related condition that tends to follow a long-term, recurring pattern.

What Is Psoriasis?

Psoriasis is a chronic, non-contagious skin condition in which the immune system speeds up the growth cycle of skin cells, causing thick, inflamed patches on the skin. These patches are often red and covered with silvery-white scales and may appear repeatedly over time.

  • It is a long-term, recurring skin condition
  • Caused by rapid buildup of skin cells
  • Commonly affects scalp, elbows, knees, and lower back
  • May cause itching, dryness, or discomfort
  • It is not infectious and does not spread by touch

Why Does Psoriasis Occur?

Psoriasis occurs due to an abnormal immune response in which the body mistakenly signals skin cells to grow and multiply much faster than normal. This rapid cell turnover leads to the buildup of thick, scaly patches on the skin.

  • Immune system dysfunction triggers excessive skin cell production
  • Genetic tendency increases the risk of developing psoriasis
  • Stress can worsen or trigger flare-ups
  • Skin injury, infections, or certain medications may aggravate symptoms
  • Cold weather and lifestyle imbalance can intensify the condition

According to Ayurveda, psoriasis is linked to the imbalance of doshas—especially Pitta and Kapha—along with toxin accumulation in the blood, which reflects on the skin.

What To Do

Managing psoriasis requires long-term care focused on calming inflammation, supporting immunity, and maintaining healthy skin through consistent daily habits.

  • Follow a balanced, anti-inflammatory diet with fresh and light foods
  • Keep the skin moisturized to reduce dryness and scaling
  • Protect the skin from extreme cold, heat, and dryness
  • Manage stress through proper sleep and relaxation practices
  • Maintain regular digestion and a disciplined daily routine
  • Use gentle, non-irritating skincare products

Ayurveda emphasizes internal balance and lifestyle correction to help reduce flare-ups and improve overall skin comfort naturally.

What To Avoid

Certain foods, habits, and lifestyle factors can aggravate psoriasis by increasing internal heat, toxins, and inflammation. Avoiding these triggers helps reduce flare-ups and supports natural healing.

  • Spicy, oily, fried, and highly processed foods
  • Excess intake of non-vegetarian and fermented foods
  • Alcohol, smoking, and stimulants
  • Late nights, irregular sleep, and disturbed daily routine
  • Stress, anger, and emotional imbalance
  • Harsh chemical-based soaps, creams, and cosmetics
  • Scratching or over-washing affected skin areas

Ayurveda advises avoiding these factors to prevent dosha aggravation and allow the skin to recover by restoring internal balance.
