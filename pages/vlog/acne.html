Acne & Pimples

Ayurvedic understanding of acne, pimples, causes, lifestyle care and long-term skin balance.

What is Acne?

Acne is a common skin condition that develops when hair follicles (pores) become blocked with excess oil (sebum), dead skin cells, and bacteria. This blockage leads to inflammation and causes different types of skin lesions such as pimples, blackheads, whiteheads, and sometimes painful red bumps or cysts.

Acne most often appears on the face, but it can also affect the chest, back, shoulders, and neck. It is commonly seen during teenage years, but many adults experience acne as well.

Why Does Acne Occur?

Acne occurs when internal and external factors disturb the normal functioning of the skin, leading to clogged pores and inflammation.

  • Poor Digestion: Improper digestion can lead to toxin buildup in the body, which may reflect on the skin as acne.
  • Hormonal Imbalance: Fluctuations in hormones increase oil production, making the skin more prone to breakouts.
  • Stress: High stress levels trigger hormonal changes that stimulate excess sebum production.
  • Wrong Food Habits: Frequent intake of oily, spicy, or junk food can worsen acne by affecting skin and digestion.

What to Do

Managing acne requires a combination of proper skin care, healthy lifestyle habits, and internal balance. Following the right steps can help control breakouts and prevent future flare-ups.

  • Keep your skin clean: Wash your face gently twice a day to remove excess oil and dirt.
  • Avoid touching or picking pimples: This can increase inflammation and cause scars.
  • Follow a healthy diet: Eat fresh fruits, vegetables, and light meals while avoiding oily and junk food.
  • Manage stress: Practice relaxation techniques like meditation or deep breathing.
  • Use suitable skincare products: Choose non-comedogenic and skin-friendly products.
  • Stay hydrated: Drink enough water to support skin detoxification.
  • Seek proper guidance: Consult a skin expert for personalized care if acne persists.

What To Avoid

Avoiding certain habits and triggers can help prevent acne from worsening and reduce the chances of new breakouts.

  • Do not touch or pick pimples: This can increase inflammation and lead to scars or dark spots.
  • Avoid oily and junk food: These foods can increase oil production and worsen acne.
  • Do not use harsh skincare products: Strong soaps and scrubs can irritate the skin and trigger breakouts.
  • Avoid heavy or pore-clogging cosmetics: Use non-comedogenic products only.
  • Do not wash your face too often: Over-cleansing can dry out the skin and increase oil production.
  • Avoid excessive stress: Stress can trigger hormonal changes that worsen acne.
  • Do not ignore persistent acne: Delaying proper care can lead to long-term marks and scars.
